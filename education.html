Program

Engineering Physics is the most competitive and academically rigorous engineering program at UBC. It is a 5 year program that combines mechatronics engineering with honors-level applied math and physics. In computer engineering I’ve gained hands-on experience developing embedded systems and machine learning applications, including firmware development, real-time system design, and computer architecture. In electronics I’ve gained experience designing, prototyping, and troubleshooting digital and analog circuits, making PCBs, and designing control systems. In mechanical engineering I’ve learned to apply advanced math and physics to analyze mechanical systems while also gaining practical skills in CAD, machining, and mechanical design.

I chose this program because I was interested in multidisciplinary fields like robotics and mechatronics engineering where integrating knowledge across mechanical, electrical, and software domains is essential. The program offers a variety of specializations totaling 18 credits of coursework and my enjoyment of low-level software projects has inspired me to specialize in embedded systems. This specilization has allow me to take additional courses in topics such as firmware, real-time system design, machine learning, and operating systems.
